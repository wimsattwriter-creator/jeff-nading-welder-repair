What's Involved

Engine work requires removing the power plant from the welder frame, complete disassembly, measurement of all wear surfaces, and precision reassembly. Kohler V-twin engines used in Miller Bobcats demand exact valve clearances, timing alignment, and torque specifications. A single missed step means the engine fails again.

Common reasons for engine failure: Low oil/neglected maintenance, overheating, worn rings and valve seals, seized bearings, broken timing components.

What's Involved

Flywheel magnets weaken or break over time, causing poor charging, no-start conditions, and erratic electrical behavior. Alternator and charging system repairs require pulling the flywheel, inspecting magnets, testing coil output, and replacing worn components. These are immediate repairs that get your machine back to work.

Common failures: Weak or broken flywheel magnets, worn slip rings, failed charging coils, bad voltage regulator.

What's Involved

The transformer is what converts generator output into welding current. When the lamination core corrodes — often from moisture entering the machine during outdoor storage — the entire transformer assembly must be rebuilt or replaced. This involves removing a component that weighs 50+ pounds, sourcing or building a replacement core, winding new primary and secondary coils, and testing output characteristics. This is the most specialized repair we perform.

Common reasons for failure: Outdoor storage without covers, condensation from temperature swings, flooding, and age. A corroded transformer core cannot be repaired — it must be replaced.

What's Involved

The control board is the brain of your welder — it regulates output current, manages engine speed, and protects components from overload. When traces burn or components fail, the entire machine goes down. Repair requires identifying the failed component, tracing the circuit, and replacing or rebuilding the damaged section. Modern boards use surface-mount components that require specialized soldering equipment.

Common reasons for failure: Power surges, overloading, moisture intrusion, component aging, and vibration-induced solder cracks.
