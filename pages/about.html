About Jeff Nading

The technician behind the work. One person. Multiple specialties. Machines restored right.

One Technician, Six Specialties

Most repair shops have different people for engines, electrical, and bodywork. Jeff does it all — because that's what it takes to properly restore a welder/generator.

When a machine comes into the shop, Jeff doesn't just fix the one thing that's broken. He inspects the entire unit — engine, generator, electrical, body, fuel system — because problems in one area often cause failures in another. A corroded connector can burn out a control board. A worn engine can destroy a stator. Everything is connected.

Specialties

  • ⚙ Engine Mechanical — Kohler twin cylinder rebuilds, valve jobs, timing, crankshaft work
  • ⚡ Stator Rewinding — Hand-wound generator stator rebuilds from bare lamination
  • 🔧 Transformer Cores — Corroded core replacement and winding rebuild
  • 💻 Circuit Board Repair — Component-level diagnostics and trace repair
  • ⛽ Electronic Diagnostics — Kohler factory software, fuel injection analysis
  • 🎨 Complete Restorations — Ground-up rebuild including paint and body

Why Professional Repair Matters

Some repairs are straightforward. Others require years of hands-on experience and specialized equipment.

Stator Rewinding

Hundreds of turns of copper wire wound by hand through numbered slots. One wrong connection and the stator is dead. No YouTube shortcut for this.

Transformer Rebuild

A corroded transformer core weighs 50+ pounds and must be completely replaced. This is heavy, specialized work requiring precise winding calculations.

Board-Level Repair

Modern control boards use surface-mount components requiring microscope work and specialized soldering equipment. A steady hand and circuit knowledge are essential.
